Off-line isolated power supply for IoT applications

Dear valued customer,


The fast growing market around the Internet of Things (IoT) covers a wide range of applications. All of them have one thing in common, namely the ability to collect and exchange data with each other. The microcontrollers, sensors, actuators, etc. embedded in each of the devices stay connected to the network at all times. They are supplied by an efficient low-power low-standby power supply for which the Infineon CoolSET™ family of integrated off-line power supply IC’s is ideally suited.

Reference design schematics

In this reference design, Infineon’s CoolSET™ ICE3RBR4765JG is used as a current-mode flyback controller. It has a built-in 650 V CoolMOS™. This application operates in Discontinuous Conduction Mode (DCM), running at 65 kHz switching frequency. The output is single 5 V 600 mA. Active burst mode operation provides extremely low standby power consumption, < 13 mW over the input voltage range 180 - 265 VAC. Low EMI is achieved by built-in frequency jitter and soft-start operation.
